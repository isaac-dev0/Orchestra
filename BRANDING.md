## What we are

An AI staff agency. Businesses rent agents that think, remember, and act like
real members of staff. The technology is invisible. The result is a person.

The first product is **Rosa** — an AI receptionist for plumbing businesses.
She answers calls and WhatsApps, qualifies jobs, books appointments, and
remembers every customer. Dave the plumber never misses a job again.

We are not a software company. We are a staffing agency that happens to run on software.

## The word "AI"

**We do not use the word "AI" in customer-facing copy unless unavoidable.**

It is understood that the agents are AI-powered. Most customers don't care how it
works — they care that it works. Saying "AI" raises questions (is it safe? will it
make mistakes? is this going to replace me?) that we don't need to answer.

Instead:
- ❌ "AI-powered receptionist"  →  ✅ "Your receptionist"
- ❌ "Automated call handling"  →  ✅ "Rosa answers your calls"
- ❌ "LLM-driven agent"         →  ✅ "Rosa"
- ❌ "AI staff platform"        →  ✅ "[Platform] — your staff, no payroll"

The one exception: "Powered by [Platform]" at the end of interactions (see Brand
Exposure below). This is acknowledgement, not a feature claim.

## Audience

### Primary — the sole trader and small team
Dave the plumber. 1–5 people. Runs everything himself. On the tools all day,
can't pick up the phone. Loses jobs every week to missed calls.

- Not technical. Does not care about AI.
- Cares about: getting his evenings back, not missing jobs, looking professional.
- WhatsApp is his office. His phone is his business.
- Age: typically 35–55.
- Decision: makes it himself, in 5 minutes, if the value is obvious.

### Secondary — the scaling small business
A roofing company with 8 staff. An electrician franchise. Past the sole-trader
stage but can't justify a full-time receptionist — or wants to add sales and
marketing support without hiring.

- Gets the ROI argument faster.
- More likely to buy a team of agents, not just one.
- May have an office manager who makes the decision.

### Who we are NOT targeting (yet)
Enterprise. Tech companies. Anyone who'd call this an "AI solution." If they'd
read TechCrunch, they're not our customer right now.

### The thing they share
Time-poor, not money-poor. They'd pay £100/month without blinking if it saved
them 3 hours of admin and one missed job a week. The value proposition must be
in their language: **time and money, not technology**.

## Voice and tone

### Three words
**Warm. Straight-talking. Reliable.**

### The spectrum
- Formal ←——●———→ Casual  (leaning casual, not sloppy)
- Cold ←————————●→ Warm  (very warm)
- Technical ←●—————→ Plain English  (almost always plain English)
- Hype ←●—————————→ Understated  (we don't hype)
- Global ←——●———————→ British  (proudly British)

### Rules

**Write like a good receptionist, not a marketer.**
Short sentences. Active voice. No jargon.

**Never oversell.**
"Rosa answers your calls" is stronger than "Rosa revolutionises your
customer communication." The first is believable. The second isn't.

**Dry humour is allowed. Forced humour isn't.**
"AI staff that actually show up" works because it's true and a bit wry.
Puns, wordplay, and exclamation marks are not our style.

**Contractions always.**
"She's available 24/7" not "She is available 24/7."
"You don't pay for holidays" not "You do not pay for holidays."

**The customer is always Dave, not a persona.**
Write as if you're talking to one specific person, not a demographic segment.

### Copy examples

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "Revolutionise your business operations with AI-powered agent automation." | "Rosa answers your calls. You get on with the job." |
| "An LLM-driven receptionist with natural language processing and calendar integration." | "She picks up your calls, asks the right questions, and books the job in your diary." |
| "Flexible subscription tiers with usage-based billing." | "One price. No surprises. Cancel whenever you like." |
| "An unexpected error has occurred. Please contact support." | "Something went wrong on our end. We're on it — try again in a moment." |
| "AI-powered 24/7 availability." | "Rosa doesn't sleep. She doesn't take holidays. She doesn't call in sick." |

## Visual identity

### Colours

| Name | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Deep Green | `#1B3A2D` | Primary brand colour. Trust, reliability, permanence. |
| Mid Green | `#2D5C45` | Buttons, links, interactive elements. |
| Warm Off-White | `#F5F0E8` | Page backgrounds. Human, not clinical. |
| Coral / Terracotta | `#C8624A` | Accent. Energy, warmth, action. CTAs, highlights. |
| Warm Almost-Black | `#1A1A18` | Body text. Warm, not harsh. |
| Light Sage | `#E8F0EB` | Secondary backgrounds, card surfaces. |

**Colour principles:**
- Deep green is the brand. It should own the hero, the nav, and the logo.
- Off-white backgrounds make the product feel approachable, not corporate.
- Coral is used sparingly — CTAs, notification badges, Rosa's avatar accent.
  It should pop, not wallpaper.
- Never use pure black (`#000000`) or pure white (`#FFFFFF`).
- Dark mode: invert thoughtfully. Deep green backgrounds with off-white text
  work well. Don't just flip colours.

### Typography

**Display / Headings — Fraunces**
- Google Fonts, free, no licensing issues.
- Warm, characterful serif. Feels like a quality local business, not a tech startup.
- Use for: hero headlines, section titles, agent names in marketing contexts.
- Weight: 700 (Bold) for headlines. 400 (Regular) for large subheadings.
- The optical size axis (`opsz`) should be used — larger sizes use the display
  variant automatically if set up correctly.

**Body — Inter or DM Sans**
- Clean, highly legible sans-serif.
- Must work at small sizes on mobile — Dave reads the dashboard in a van.
- Use for: all body copy, UI labels, navigation, form fields.
- Weight: 400 regular, 500 medium for emphasis. Avoid 700 in body text.

**Mono — JetBrains Mono**
- For: job IDs, timestamps, booking references, any data that needs to feel precise.
- Used sparingly in the dashboard. Not in marketing.

**Scale (approximate)**
```
Display:   48–72px  Fraunces 700
H1:        36–48px  Fraunces 700
H2:        28–36px  Fraunces 400
H3:        20–24px  Inter 500
Body:      16px     Inter 400
Small:     14px     Inter 400
Label:     12px     Inter 500 uppercase tracked
Mono:      14px     JetBrains Mono 400
```

### Illustration style

**Abstract illustration — not photography.**

We do not use stock photography of people, offices, or tools. We use abstract
illustration that communicates ideas through shape, colour, and movement rather
than literal representation.

**Direction:**
- Organic shapes, not geometric grids
- Warm palette (greens, off-whites, coral accents)
- Suggests motion and activity without being literal
- Could represent: a conversation, a phone call, a booking, a busy day
- Think: Linear, Lottie-style animations for key moments (booking confirmed,
  Rosa answering a call)

**What to avoid:**
- Flat cartoon people (too startup-generic)
- Photorealistic 3D (too cold)
- Stock illustration libraries (too generic)
- Anything that looks like it came from Undraw (too recognisable)

**For the hackathon:** use a consistent abstract shape language in the Nuxt
dashboard. Post-hackathon: commission a proper illustration set.

### Agent avatars

**Illustrated characters — not photos, not initials.**

Each agent has a distinct illustrated avatar that conveys their personality
at a glance. Think Notion-style characters but with more personality and
warmth — friendly, approachable, clearly distinct from each other.

**Avatar principles:**
- Each character is immediately readable at 40×40px (dashboard list view)
  and looks good at 200×200px (agent profile page)
- Distinct colour accent per agent (see agent profiles below)
- Same illustration style across all agents — they're a team
- Expressions should be warm and engaged, not neutral or corporate
- No photorealism. No stock art.

**Production path:**
- MVP / hackathon: use stylised initial-based avatars with agent colour
- Post-hackathon: commission via Midjourney or a freelance illustrator
  using the brief above as the style guide

## Brand exposure — "Powered by [Platform]"

Every interaction Rosa has ends with a closing WhatsApp message to the customer.
At the bottom of that message:

```
─────────────────────
Powered by [Platform]
```

This appears:
- After a booking is confirmed
- After an enquiry is resolved
- After an escalation ("Dave will call you back shortly")

**Why this works:**
- Customer is at peak satisfaction — just got sorted
- It's acknowledgement, not advertising
- It creates brand recognition without Dave having to promote us
- It's opt-out-able for white-label customers on premium tiers (post-hackathon)

**What it is not:**
- It is not a marketing message
- It does not include a URL or call to action in the initial version
- It does not say "AI" or describe what [Platform] is

## The agents

All agents share a common personality foundation, then have their own character
on top.

### Shared personality foundation
Every agent is:
- **Friendly** — warm, approachable, never cold or transactional
- **Bubbly** — there's energy and life in how they communicate
- **Personable** — they can have a bit of a conversation, they're not just
  processing requests. If Dave's customer mentions the weather, they can
  acknowledge it.
- **On track** — they bring conversations back to purpose naturally, without
  being abrupt. They don't get lost in small talk.
- **Detail-oriented** — they get the specifics right. The right postcode,
  the right time slot, the right job type. They confirm before acting.
- **Honest about limits** — they don't guess. If they don't know, they say so
  and find out rather than making something up.

### Rosa — Receptionist
**Colour accent:** Coral / Terracotta (`#C8624A`)
**Voice:** Friendly, efficient, detail-oriented
**Personality:** Rosa is the first person a customer speaks to. She makes them
feel like they've called the right place. She's quick without being rushed,
warm without being over-familiar. She knows when a job is urgent and when it
isn't, and she acts accordingly. She never makes a customer feel like a problem.

**Rosa's three words:** Warm. Efficient. Reassuring.

**What Rosa sounds like:**
> "Hi! You've reached Dave's Plumbing — I'm Rosa. What can I help you with today?"

> "A dripping tap — no problem, that's an easy one for Dave. Can I just grab
> your postcode so I can check we cover your area?"

> "Thursday at 10 works perfectly. I'll pop that in the diary now. Can you
> give me the full address? And just so you know, Dave will be with you
> between 10 and 10:30 — you'll get a reminder the evening before."

**What Rosa does not sound like:**
> "I have successfully processed your appointment request and will now
> initiate a calendar booking on your behalf."

## Naming system for agents

As the agent roster grows, names should feel like a real, diverse team.
Naming principles:

- **Real first names only** — not "Aria" or "Nova" or anything that sounds like
  an AI assistant. Real names that a person would have.
- **Diverse** — the team should represent a range of backgrounds through naming.
- **Role-appropriate personality, not role-appropriate name** — Daisy handles
  legal, not because "Daisy" sounds legal, but because the character behind the
  name does. Don't name agents after their roles.
- **One name per role** — there is only one Rosa. There is only one Mark.
  The names become the product.
